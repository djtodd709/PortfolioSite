Planet Zoo - Burrows

Dec 2021

After creating a mechanic for meerkats to dig tunnels in an earlier release, the team wanted to expand on the concept of animals going underground. This was realised in the form of burrows, a place for certain animals to rest, give birth, and hide from adverse weather. Here's some of the details of my implementation.

Features

  • Overriding existing AI for eligible species
  • Burrow queueing system for entering/exiting
  • Movement and pathfinding for animals while underground
  • Spawning of new AI agents with modified behaviour
  • Bespoke underground camera

Animals can move around in the burrow and perform many behaviours, leaving once they need something above ground, or get restless. Newborns will wait for the mother to leave the burrow, before all following her out. Burrow entrances can be placed anywhere in the habitat with enough space, with the underground chamber automatically positioning itself in a valid location if possible.

All Animal AI states have modified behaviour when inside a burrow.

I avoided the need for new animations, making the mechanic easily extendable

Development

Navigation was handled by preexisting, tested solutions, thanks to my code and discussions with our modelling team about including a preset navigation mesh in the burrow interior. By also discussing the entrance geometry in-depth, we also eliminated the need for any specific burrow animations, teleporting the animal from the entrance to the burrow interior, disabling any problematic physics and IK along the way.
I also worked on our camera code to allow players to see into their burrows via an in-universe webcam, which they could cast onto billboards they placed throughout their park. The camera came with the stretch goal of a small investigation behaviour, for our more curious species. This goal was met, alongside all the other stretch goals for this feature.

What I'd Do Next Time

Considering how the final product turned out, I see a lot of potential in a system that was more generalised. I planned out an "underground area animals can go to sleep/shelter/give birth" when I could've planned a "____ area animals can go to ____", something more extendable for the future. We haven't needed a similar system since, but a more generic system always makes it easier for designers to suggest ways to use it.
