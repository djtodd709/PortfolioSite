Planet Zoo - Burrows

I've been a part of the Planet Zoo team since August 2020, and in that time I've done a great deal. From animal behaviour code to experience with our render system, the video below provides a brief overview of my accomplishments, and how I've contributed to the 8 DLC releases I've worked on.

Summary

I've been a gameplay programmer during 8 DLC releases for Planet Zoo, a simulation game allowing players to build and manage a zoo. I'm currently the primary coder responible for animal behaviour, obviously a key part of a game with a main gameplay loop of building habitats for animals to interact with.

With many new animals performing various types of actions, my role has required interaction between many different departments at once to coordinate how exactly a feature comes to fruition. This typically includes:

  • Discussions with the design team to establish how we implement their ideas in a technically feasible way
  • Planning out features with the production team, to reduce loads on departments like art and animation
  • Working with the asset creation teams to ensure everything created is fit for purpose and won't introduce technical hurdles later on

Implementing these features requires an understanding of our in-house game engine, as well as the ability to work with the multiple programming languages we use:

  • C++
  • Lua
  • HLSL

I've also been trusted to take on some of the responsibilities of other departments, such as making graphical changes, normally handled by our render team. This has furthered my inter-departmental communication skills, to the point where I'm the project's most direct communication link outside of production to both of Frontier's core Animation and Render programming teams. I'm also regularly chosen as a programming representative by our community team for livestreams and other social events.
